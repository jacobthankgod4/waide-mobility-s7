Total people entering your venue each month

🔍 How to Find This Data:
  • Security cameras: Most accurate method - count entries/exits
  • Door counters: Automatic sensors at entrances
  • WiFi analytics: Track unique device connections
  • POS systems: Transaction data + conversion rate estimates
  • Manual counting: Sample days extrapolated to monthly

💡 Pro Tip: If you don't know exact numbers, use your busiest day's count × 20 (for monthly estimate). Most venues underestimate their actual foot traffic by 30-40%.

Average amount each visitor spends
• Electronics: $80-150
• Restaurant/Food: $15-25
• Grocery stores: $45-75
• Department stores: $25-50
• Luxury retail: $100-300
• Convenience stores: $8-15
• Pharmacies: $20-35
🧮 How to Calculate:
Total Monthly Revenue ÷ Total Monthly Visitors = Average Spend

Example: $150,000 revenue ÷ 10,000 visitors = $15 average spend per visitor

⚠️ Common Mistakes:
  • Using average transaction value instead (this excludes non-buyers)
  • Not including all revenue streams (services, add-ons, etc.)
  • Forgetting to account for returns and refunds

💡 Pro Tip: If you only know your average transaction value, divide it by your conversion rate to get average spend per visitor. For example: $50 transaction ÷ 20% conversion = $10 per visitor.

Percentage of visitors who make a purchase
• Luxury stores: 5-15%
• Grocery stores: 60-80%
• Electronics: 8-15%
• Clothing stores: 12-25%
• Restaurants: 85-95%
• Pharmacies: 40-60%
• Department stores: 15-30%
🧮 How to Calculate:
(Number of Transactions ÷ Total Visitors) × 100 = Conversion Rate %

Example: 500 transactions ÷ 2,500 visitors × 100 = 20% conversion rate

📊 Factors That Influence Conversion:
  • Navigation ease: Lost customers don't buy
  • Staff availability: Assistance increases conversion
  • Product findability: Can't buy what you can't find
  • Store layout: Intuitive flow improves conversion
  • Wait times: Long queues reduce conversion
