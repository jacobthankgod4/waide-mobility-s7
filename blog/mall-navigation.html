The $2.3 Billion Problem

Every weekend, millions of African shoppers walk into malls with money to spend. But here's what happens:

They can't find the store they're looking for. They wander aimlessly for 23 minutes. They get frustrated. They leave—without buying anything.

That's ₦2.3 billion in lost retail sales across Africa. Every. Single. Month.

But it's not just about lost sales. It's about the psychology of shopping. When customers can't find what they're looking for, three things happen:

  • Impulse buying drops 47% — No browsing = No spontaneous purchases
  • Dwell time decreases 32% — Frustrated shoppers leave faster
  • Return visits fall 28% — Bad experience = Lost loyalty

The Shopping Psychology You're Missing

Retail isn't just about products. It's about the journey. And when that journey is frustrating, wallets stay closed.
