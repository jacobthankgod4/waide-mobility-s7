How It Works

1

Venue Floorplan Submission

Venues submit their floorplans, a crucial first step in building a map that enables accessible navigation and independence for everyone.

2

Digitization & Georeferencing

We digitize the floorplan and georeference it to Earth's coordinates using GIS indoor mapping tools. We then add points of interest and routing for seamless navigation.

3

App Integration

This digitized interactive floorplan is integrated into our app, providing users with accurate, turn-by-turn directions and a new level of confidence and freedom.

Frequently Asked Questions

Waide Mobility is a cutting-edge platform that reimagines indoor and outdoor navigation for people with disabilities, particularly the visually impaired in Africa. Using AI-powered technology, it transforms smartphones into assistive devices, enabling users to navigate complex spaces seamlessly without costly specialized equipment.
Indoor navigation refers to the use of technology to provide accurate directions and location-based services within buildings where traditional GPS doesn't work well. It involves digital maps, sensors, and apps to guide users through malls, airports, hospitals, and other venues.
The process begins with venues submitting their floorplans. Our team digitizes these plans, georeferences them to real-world coordinates using GIS tools, and adds interactive elements like points of interest, routing paths, and accessibility features to create comprehensive digital environments.
Waide integrates with users' smartphones to provide real-time navigation. After selecting a destination, the app uses AR to display virtual guides, offers audio descriptions, and provides haptic feedback. It combines outdoor GPS with indoor positioning for continuous guidance.
Waide is designed for visually impaired individuals, but also helps elderly people, families with children, tourists, and anyone who needs reliable navigation in unfamiliar or complex indoor environments, promoting independence and confidence.
Absolutely. Waide adheres to WCAG accessibility standards, featuring intuitive interfaces, voice commands, screen reader compatibility, and customizable options to ensure it's usable by people with diverse abilities, including those with motor or cognitive challenges.
Waide supports a wide range of indoor spaces including shopping malls, airports, hospitals, office buildings, universities, hotels, convention centers, sports venues, museums, train stations, libraries, warehouses, manufacturing facilities, data centers, parking garages, and even cruise ships.
Our pricing is flexible: $299/month for small spaces (up to 5,000 sq ft), $1,199/month for medium spaces (up to 50,000 sq ft), and custom pricing for large complexes. We offer a 20% discount for yearly plans to make it more affordable.
Getting started is easy. Reach out via email at waidemobility@gmail.com or use the contact form in our pricing section. We'll discuss your specific needs, venue type, and provide a tailored solution, including a free trial to experience the platform.
Key features include a mobile-first platform with zero infrastructure costs, immersive AR navigation for engaging experiences, global cloud infrastructure for reliability, real-time data visualization with ERP integrations, and smart building features like room booking and IoT connectivity.
AR navigation in Waide uses augmented reality to overlay interactive digital elements onto the real world through your smartphone camera. This creates virtual guides that appear as friendly characters, providing step-by-step directions, highlighting obstacles, and offering contextual information to enhance navigation.
Waide prioritizes data security with enterprise-grade encryption, secure cloud storage on global infrastructure, compliance with international data protection regulations, and regular security audits. User data is anonymized and never shared without consent.
Waide is optimized for emerging markets with offline navigation capabilities, battery-efficient design, and multi-language support including local African languages. It works in areas with limited connectivity and supports climate adaptation by providing reliable guidance during extreme weather events for children, elderly, and vulnerable communities.
